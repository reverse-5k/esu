Level 2 vs. DC Fast Charging

The right charger type depends on how long vehicles typically stay at your location. We'll help you choose based on actual dwell time, not guesswork.

Level 2 (240V)

20–30 mi/hr

Best for locations where vehicles stay 2+ hours: workplaces, apartments, hotels, and restaurants. Low installation cost, broad compatibility with all EVs.

  • Best for all current EVs
  • Lower infrastructure cost
  • Scalable to 4, 8, 16+ ports
  • Load management available
  • Best ROI for most businesses

DC Fast Charger (DCFC)

100–250 mi/hr

Best for locations where vehicles stop briefly: gas station replacements, highway corridors, and high-turnover retail. Higher infrastructure cost, maximum throughput.

  • Full charge in 20–45 minutes
  • Ideal for high-turnover locations
  • Requires 480V 3-phase service
  • Supports CCS/CHAdeMO/NACS
  • Revenue-generating potential

Federal Tax Credits Can Cover Up to 30% of Installation Costs

The IRS Alternative Fuel Infrastructure Tax Credit (IRC 30C) covers 30% of commercial EV charging equipment and installation costs, up to $100,000 per location for businesses in low-income or rural areas, and $30,000 for others. We provide all the cost documentation needed to claim it.

Additional Florida and FPL incentives may be available depending on your location and project scope. Ask us about what applies to your project during the estimate call.
