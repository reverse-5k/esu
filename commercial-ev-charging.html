Level 2 vs. DC Fast Charging: Which Is Right for Your Property?

The right charger type depends on how long vehicles typically stay at your location. We'll walk you through the tradeoffs during the free estimate — the answer isn't always obvious.

Level 2 (240V AC)

Best for Most Businesses
20–30 mi/hr

Best for locations where vehicles park for 2+ hours: workplaces, apartments, hotels, and restaurants. Lower infrastructure cost, broad EV compatibility, and the most cost-effective option for the majority of commercial properties.

  • Compatible with 100% of current EVs
  • Lower infrastructure and hardware cost
  • Easily scalable to 4, 8, 16+ ports
  • Load management available to reduce demand charges
  • Best ROI for most South Florida commercial properties

DC Fast Charger (DCFC)

High-Turnover Locations
100–250 mi/hr

Best for locations where vehicles stop briefly — gas station alternatives, highway corridors, and high-turnover retail. Higher infrastructure cost, but maximum throughput and revenue potential from charging fees.

  • Full charge in 20–45 minutes
  • Highest throughput per stall
  • Requires 480V 3-phase electrical service
  • Supports CCS, CHAdeMO, and NACS connectors
  • Revenue generation model available

Federal Tax Credits Can Cover Up to 30% of Installation Costs

The IRS Alternative Fuel Infrastructure Tax Credit (IRC Section 30C) covers 30% of the cost of commercial EV charging equipment and installation — up to $100,000 per property for businesses in qualifying census tracts, or $30,000 for all others. We provide itemized cost documentation formatted for IRS Form 8911 as part of every commercial EV project.

Additional FPL incentives and Florida-specific programs may apply depending on project scope and location. Ask about what applies to your specific property during the estimate call.
